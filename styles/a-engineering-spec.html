02 — OPTIMIZATION

Six levers for every engagement

Most teams combine three or four. The audit ranks which dominate your spend surface and projects savings before any commitment. All ranges are illustrative; real targets determined after invoice audit and workload review.

Lever Technique Typical Impact Effort
01 Model routing — Each request classified by complexity and routed to the cheapest model that meets your quality bar. 30–50% Medium
02 Semantic caching — Near-duplicate prompts fingerprinted with embeddings; identical answers served from sub-10ms cache. 20–40% Low
03 Prompt compression — System prompts audited for redundancy. Context compressed with LLMLingua-style techniques. Every change A/B tested. 15–30% Medium
04 Batch & async pricing — Non-interactive workloads routed to batch endpoints with SLO-aware queueing for time-sensitive paths. 10–50% High
05 Provider arbitrage — Identical capability often costs 2–3× more at one provider. Route by capability-per-dollar, not SDK inertia. 20–35% Low
06 Fallback chains — Smart retries and tiered fallbacks beat worst-case over-provisioning. Maintain SLO without paying flagship prices on every call. 5–15% Medium

06 — FAQ

Common questions

+ What is FinOps for LLM?
FinOps for LLM brings the FinOps Foundation framework — visibility, attribution, optimization, and accountability — to LLM and GenAI spend. It includes per-feature and per-team chargeback, anomaly detection, budget governance, and continuous optimization of model routing, caching, and prompts.
+ How much can we save?
Savings depend on your workload shape, provider mix, cacheability, and quality constraints. The audit identifies which optimizations are material before any implementation. Illustrative ranges: 30–50% from routing, 20–40% from caching, 15–30% from compression. Real targets are locked after audit.
+ Which providers do you support?
OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini & Vertex AI, AWS Bedrock, Azure OpenAI, Groq, Together, Mistral, Cohere, Fireworks, Replicate, and most open-source endpoints. Multi-provider deployments typically have the largest savings surface.
+ Does FinOps LLM support chargeback and showback?
Yes. Token-level attribution to providers, models, features, teams, and customer cohorts. Monthly chargeback and showback exported to NetSuite, QuickBooks, CSV, or API. Custom dimensions supported.
+ How do you handle customer data?
Read-only by default. Billing and usage data is enough for most attribution work. Prompt and output data is only accessed with explicit customer approval for specific optimizations that require it. NDA and DPA available on request.
+ How long does implementation take?
Attribution and dashboards go live in under a week. Optimization implementation takes 3–5 weeks; savings appear on the first full provider invoice after go-live.
+ Will optimization hurt output quality?
No. Every routing, caching, and compression change is A/B tested against production for seven days minimum before promotion. Regressions auto-rollback. Quality is monitored continuously alongside cost.
