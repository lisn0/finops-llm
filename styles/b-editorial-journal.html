01. The platform

Four pillars of AI FinOps.

The FinOps Foundation framework—visibility, attribution, optimization, and accountability—adapts directly to token economics. Every dollar of LLM spend becomes auditable, attributable, and optimizable. Same discipline. Different unit of cost.

Pillar 01

Visibility

Token-level cost data ingested from every provider. Reconciled hourly. Filterable by provider, model, feature, team, customer, and environment.

  • OpenAI, Anthropic, Bedrock, Gemini, Azure, Groq, Together
  • Hourly ingestion, sub-5-minute reconciliation lag
  • Read-only by default; NDA and DPA on request
Pillar 02

Attribution & chargeback

Every token mapped to a feature, team, and customer cohort. Monthly chargeback and showback, exported to your finance system or as CSV.

  • Custom dimensions: feature, team, tier, region
  • Cohort spend analysis and customer-tier P&L
  • NetSuite, QuickBooks, CSV, or API
Pillar 03

Anomaly detection

Real-time alerts when spend, latency, or quality deviates from a feature's rolling baseline. Slack, PagerDuty, email—with optional auto-throttle.

  • Per-feature rolling baselines, seasonality-aware
  • Slack, PagerDuty, email, or webhook alerts
  • Optional auto-throttle and budget enforcement
Pillar 04

Automated optimization

Model routing, semantic caching, and prompt compression deployed behind feature flags, A/B tested for seven days minimum, graduated only on quality wins.

  • Routing tree with per-request quality classification
  • Semantic cache with sub-10ms hit latency
  • Quality SLO enforcement and auto-rollback
02. Optimization

Six levers we pull in every engagement.

Most teams combine three or four. The audit ranks which dominate your spend surface and projects savings before any commitment.

No. Lever Description Savings range
01 Model routing Request-level classification routes to the cheapest model meeting your quality bar. Reasoning to flagships; classification to small fast models. 30–50%
02 Semantic caching Near-duplicate prompts fingerprinted with embeddings; identical answers served from sub-10ms cache. 20–40%
03 Prompt compression System prompts audited for redundancy. Examples deduplicated. Retrieved context compressed with LLMLingua-style techniques. 15–30%
04 Batch & async pricing Non-interactive workloads routed to batch endpoints (up to 50% discount) with SLO-aware queueing. 10–50%
05 Provider arbitrage Identical capability often costs 2–3× more at one provider. We route by capability-per-dollar. 20–35%
06 Fallback chains Smart retries and tiered fallbacks avoid over-provisioning while maintaining SLO. 5–15%

06. Questions

Common questions.

Missing something? Email hello@finopsllm.com—we respond within one business day.

What is FinOps for LLM?
FinOps for LLM brings the FinOps Foundation framework—visibility, attribution, optimization, and accountability—to LLM and GenAI spend. It includes per-feature and per-team chargeback, anomaly detection, budget governance, and continuous optimization of model routing, caching, and prompts.
What does FinOps LLM do?
FinOps LLM is the platform purpose-built for LLM cost intelligence. We provide real-time spend attribution across OpenAI, Anthropic, Bedrock, and Gemini, plus automated optimization (routing, caching, compression) and monthly reconciliation against provider invoices.
How is pricing structured?
Two models. Performance—free audit, then 15–25% of verified monthly savings. Platform—flat tiers from $1,500/month for self-serve attribution and analytics. Most customers start on Performance.
How much can we save?
Typical reduction in the first full billing cycle after implementation is 38–68%, depending on architecture and traffic mix. The audit quantifies what's material for your workload before any commitment.
Which providers are supported?
OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini & Vertex AI, AWS Bedrock, Azure OpenAI, Groq, Together, Mistral, Cohere, Fireworks, Replicate, and most open-source endpoints. Multi-provider deployments typically have the largest savings surface.
Does FinOps LLM support chargeback and showback?
Yes. Token-level attribution to providers, models, features, teams, and customer cohorts. Monthly chargeback and showback exported to NetSuite, QuickBooks, CSV, or API. Custom dimensions supported.
How do you handle customer data?
Read-only by default. Billing and usage data is enough for most attribution work. Prompt and output data is only accessed with explicit customer approval for specific optimizations that require it. NDA and DPA available on request.
How long does implementation take?
Attribution and dashboards go live in under a week. Optimization implementation takes 3–5 weeks; savings appear on the first full provider invoice after go-live.
